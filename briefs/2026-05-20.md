# Daily Research Brief — 2026-05-20

## Key Themes & Trends
The current research landscape in AI/ML is focused on developing agentic world models [Agentic World Modeling], which enable agents to understand and interact with their environment. Another key theme is the development of trading agents that operate in real-world scenarios, highlighting the need for explainability in AI decision-making [TradingAgents]. Additionally, there is a trend towards constructing composite functions that leverage remote search tools to improve agent performance [AgentScope].

## Tensions & Conflicts
A tension exists between the need for explainability in AI decision-making and the complexity of real-world scenarios. For instance, trading agents operating in real-world markets require explainable AI to ensure transparency and trust [TradingAgents]. However, the development of agentic world models [Agentic World Modeling] may lead to increased complexity, making it challenging to provide clear explanations for AI-driven decisions. Furthermore, the integration of multiple tasks and tools, such as those described in [AgentScope], can exacerbate this tension.

## Open Questions & Gaps
There are several open questions and gaps in the current research landscape. For example, how can agentic world models [Agentic World Modeling] be developed to provide transparent and explainable decision-making in complex scenarios? Additionally, what are the key challenges and limitations of constructing composite functions that leverage remote search tools [AgentScope], and how can these be addressed? The application of AI in areas like computational linguistics [AI-Trader] and robotic process automation [OpenHands] also raises questions about the potential for agentic process automation and its implications for future research.
