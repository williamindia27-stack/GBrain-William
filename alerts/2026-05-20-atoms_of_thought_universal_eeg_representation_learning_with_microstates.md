## Summary

**What it is:** This paper introduces a novel approach to electroencephalogram (EEG) representation learning using microstates, which are the building blocks of brain activity patterns at a microscopic time scale.
**Key contribution:** The authors propose a universal microstate tokenizer that clusters continuous EEG signals into sequences of discrete microstates, allowing for a simple yet effective EEG representation that can be adopted across various downstream tasks.
**Main finding:** The experimental results show that EEG representation learning with microstates outperforms traditional time-domain and frequency-domain features in tasks such as sleep staging, emotion recognition, and motor imagery classification, offering greater interpretability and scalability.
