## Overview
The paper addresses the problem of world modeling in AI systems, which is crucial for agents to accomplish goals through sustained interaction. The ability to model environment dynamics is a central bottleneck, and the term "world model" has different meanings across research communities. This paper provides a common language to align communities and charts a path from passive next-step prediction toward world models that can simulate and reshape environments. The paper synthesizes over 400 works and summarizes more than 100 representative systems, providing a roadmap for agentic world modeling.

## Method
The paper introduces a "levels×laws" taxonomy, which defines three capability levels: L1 Predictor, L2 Simulator, and L3 Evolver. The taxonomy is organized along two axes, with the first axis defining the capability levels and the second axis identifying four governing-law regimes (physical, digital, social, and scientific). The authors analyze methods, failure modes, and evaluation practices across level-regime pairs, proposing decision-centric evaluation principles and a minimal reproducible evaluation package.

## Key Results
* The paper synthesizes over 400 works and summarizes more than 100 representative systems, covering model-based reinforcement learning, video generation, web and GUI agents, multi-agent social simulation, and AI-driven scientific discovery.
