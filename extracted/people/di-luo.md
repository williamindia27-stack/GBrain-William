## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Di Luo is a researcher at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology whose work focuses on the application of large language models to financial trading and multi-agent systems. Their research explores how LLM-powered agents can be organized into specialized roles—such as fundamental analysts, sentiment analysts, and technical analysts—to create collaborative trading environments that mirror professional trading firms. In the TradingAgents framework, Luo and colleagues demonstrate that combining structured outputs with natural language dialogue among specialized agents yields measurable improvements in cumulative returns, Sharpe ratio, and maximum drawdown compared to baseline models. The framework represents an innovative approach to addressing limitations in single-agent and traditional multi-agent trading systems by leveraging domain-specific language models and collaborative decision-making processes. Luo's work has implications for enhancing the explainability and transparency of algorithmic trading decisions while advancing the broader application of multi-agent systems in high-stakes financial domains.
