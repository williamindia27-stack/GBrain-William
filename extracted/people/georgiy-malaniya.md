## Overview


<!-- bio-agent -->
Georgiy Malaniya is a researcher at the Center for Engineering Systems and Sciences specializing in reinforcement learning and policy optimization. His work addresses the fundamental challenge of training RL policies efficiently by developing an agency transferring model-free policy enhancement technique that leverages pre-existing baseline policies to accelerate learning. The method employs an arbitration module that strategically governs action selection between a learning policy and a baseline policy, gradually transferring control to the learning agent while maintaining performance guarantees. His empirical results demonstrate that this approach matches or exceeds the performance of established algorithms such as SAC, PPO, and TD3 on continuous-control benchmarks while outperforming residual reinforcement learning methods in goal-reaching rates. Malaniya's research has practical significance for real-world applications in robotics, logistics, and finance, where functional but suboptimal policies are already available and improved training efficiency is critical for deployment.
