## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Kevin Qinghong Lin is a researcher affiliated with the University of Oxford whose work focuses on world modeling for autonomous agents and artificial intelligence systems. He co-authored "Agentic World Modeling," a comprehensive synthesis of over 400 works that introduces a "levels×laws" taxonomy organizing world modeling capabilities across three levels (Predictor, Simulator, Evolver) and four governing-law regimes (physical, digital, social, and scientific). His research identifies critical failure modes and evaluation practices across different capability-regime pairs, proposing decision-centric evaluation principles and minimal reproducible evaluation packages to advance the field. Lin's contributions extend to analyzing diverse applications including model-based reinforcement learning, video generation, web and GUI agents, multi-agent social simulation, and AI-driven scientific discovery. His work provides a unifying language across research communities and establishes architectural guidance for developing world models that can simulate and reshape environments, enabling agents to accomplish sustained goal-directed interaction.
