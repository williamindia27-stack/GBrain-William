## Overview


<!-- bio-agent -->
Sinan Ibrahim is a reinforcement learning researcher at the Center for Engineering Systems and Sciences who specializes in improving training efficiency through policy transfer and adaptive learning mechanisms. His work focuses on leveraging existing baseline policies to accelerate reinforcement learning development, as demonstrated in his research on agency transferring model-free policy enhancement techniques that employ arbitration modules to govern the gradual transition of control from a baseline policy to a learning policy. His contributions demonstrate that this approach achieves competitive or superior performance compared to established methods like SAC, PPO, and TD3 on continuous-control benchmarks while maintaining higher goal-reaching rates throughout training. Ibrahim's research yields standalone neural networks capable of operating without baseline support, with explicit theoretical lower bounds for goal-reaching probability and empirical advantages over residual reinforcement learning approaches. His work addresses a critical practical challenge in deploying reinforcement learning systems, particularly in robotics, logistics, and finance where functional but suboptimal policies already exist. Ibrahim's research vision centers on making reinforcement learning more accessible and efficient in real-world applications by intelligently combining human-designed or legacy policies with machine learning-based policy improvement.
