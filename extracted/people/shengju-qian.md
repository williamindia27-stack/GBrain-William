## Overview


<!-- bio-agent -->
Shengju Qian is a computer science researcher at LIGHTSPEED specializing in the evaluation and development of vision-language model game agents. He is a core contributor to OmniGameArena, a unified benchmark for VLM game agents that spans Solo, PvP, and Coop game regimes built on Unreal Engine 5, addressing significant gaps in existing evaluation frameworks for multimodal AI systems. His work introduces the Improvement Dynamics Curve harness, which enables systematic assessment of how VLM agents adapt and refine their performance through autonomous reflection across multiple interaction rounds. Through comprehensive evaluation of heterogeneous agents including commercial and open-weight VLMs, he demonstrates that peak performance often emerges mid-optimization trajectory rather than at convergence, revealing important insights about learning dynamics in interactive environments. His research contributes to establishing more realistic and comprehensive evaluation methodologies for vision-language models in complex interactive settings, with implications for developing more adaptive AI agents for real-world applications.
