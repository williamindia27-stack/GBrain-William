## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Zhefan Rao is a researcher specializing in artificial intelligence and world modeling for autonomous agents. He co-authored "Agentic World Modeling," a comprehensive synthesis of over 400 works that introduces a "levels×laws" taxonomy organizing world modeling systems across three capability levels—Predictor, Simulator, and Evolver—and four governing-law regimes spanning physical, digital, social, and scientific domains. His work analyzes failure modes and evaluation practices across diverse applications including model-based reinforcement learning, video generation, web and GUI agents, and multi-agent social simulation. Rao and his collaborators propose decision-centric evaluation principles and a minimal reproducible evaluation package to advance standardization in the field. His research aims to establish common language across fragmented research communities and chart a path toward world models capable of simulating and reshaping environments to enable sustained agent goal accomplishment.
**Affiliation:** [[hong-kong-university-of-science-and-technology|Hong Kong University of Science and Technology]]
