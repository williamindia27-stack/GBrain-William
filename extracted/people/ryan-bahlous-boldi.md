## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Ryan Bahlous-Boldi is a computer science researcher at MIT working on reinforcement learning and language model optimization. His research focuses on improving test-time search performance through novel training approaches, particularly Vector Policy Optimization (VPO), which trains models to generate diverse solutions by anticipating multiple reward functions and spanning the Pareto frontier of possible answers. In his work on VPO, he demonstrates that multi-answer generation combined with stochastic reward scalarizations substantially outperforms scalar baselines across benchmarks including coding tasks, reasoning, and multi-hop question answering, even unlocking problems that standard methods cannot solve at any candidate budget. His approach addresses a fundamental challenge in post-training language models: the tendency toward low-entropy response distributions that limit exploration in downstream search procedures. Bahlous-Boldi's research agenda centers on designing training methods that balance diversity and quality, with implications for how language models can be optimized for complex tasks requiring both exploration and exploitation.
