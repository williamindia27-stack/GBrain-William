## Overview


<!-- bio-agent -->
Shilong Liu is a researcher at Princeton University working in machine learning and natural language processing, with a focus on adaptive learning systems for foundation models. His research addresses the practical challenge of deploying self-improving agents that can handle diverse, heterogeneous input streams across multiple datasets and domains without catastrophic interference. In his work on Eevee, a test-time prompt learning framework, he developed a router-prompt co-evolution strategy that enables specialized prompts and intelligent routing mechanisms to work in tandem, achieving substantial improvements over baseline models like Qwen3-4B-Instruct and DeepSeek-V3.2 while maintaining cumulative retention gains across incremental multi-benchmark settings. His approach demonstrates significant competitive advantages over existing state-of-the-art methods, outperforming comparable frameworks by up to 48 percent while avoiding excessive prompt expansion. Liu's work is motivated by the real-world need for models to continuously refine their behavior through interaction with heterogeneous task streams after deployment, contributing to more robust and adaptive artificial intelligence systems.
