## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Ryan Rao is a computer scientist at Carnegie Mellon University working at the intersection of multimodal learning, video understanding, and human-AI collaboration. He contributes to research on precise video language understanding through frameworks that combine structured specifications with human oversight mechanisms. In the CHAI framework, he participates in developing critique-based correction methods that leverage model-generated pre-captions and human feedback to produce improved video descriptions with fine-grained control over motion, camera dynamics, and cinematography. His work demonstrates that explicit preference and critique supervision can enable open-source video-language models to outperform closed-source alternatives while maintaining scalability through efficient human-AI collaboration. The research addresses critical challenges in text-to-video generation and professional-grade video content creation by establishing foundations for more precise language control in vision-language systems.
