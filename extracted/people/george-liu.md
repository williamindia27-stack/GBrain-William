## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
George Liu is a computer scientist at Carnegie Mellon University specializing in multimodal learning and video understanding. He contributes to research on precise video language understanding through the development of frameworks that bridge the gap between visual content and linguistic description. His work on the CHAI framework demonstrates expertise in human-AI collaboration, specifically in designing critique-based oversight systems that improve video captioning through model-generated pre-captions refined by human feedback. Liu's research addresses fundamental limitations in current video-language models, particularly their struggle with fine-grained aspects such as subject motion and camera dynamics, while leveraging their strengths in subject appearance and scene context. His work enables professional-grade text-to-video generation systems with precise language control, demonstrating the practical applications of improved video language understanding for content creation workflows. Through collaborative research at Carnegie Mellon, Liu contributes to advancing open-source multimodal models that can outperform closed-source alternatives with appropriate supervision strategies.
