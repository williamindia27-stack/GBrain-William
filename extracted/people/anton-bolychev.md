## Overview


<!-- bio-agent -->
Anton Bolychev is a reinforcement learning researcher at the Center for Engineering Systems and Sciences whose work focuses on improving the efficiency and practical applicability of RL training methods. He develops novel approaches to policy enhancement by leveraging pre-existing baseline policies through intelligent arbitration mechanisms, as exemplified in his work on agency transferring model-free policy enhancement techniques. His method introduces a switching module that gradually transfers control from a baseline policy to a learning policy, enabling more efficient training while maintaining performance guarantees throughout the learning process. Bolychev's empirical work demonstrates that his approach achieves superior goal-reaching rates and training efficiency compared to established methods such as SAC, PPO, and TD3 on continuous-control benchmarks. His research addresses practical constraints in real-world applications such as robotics and finance, where functional but suboptimal policies already exist and can serve as valuable learning scaffolds. Through rigorous theoretical analysis and comprehensive empirical validation, Bolychev advances the field's understanding of how to effectively combine baseline and learned policies for accelerated and robust reinforcement learning.
