## Overview


<!-- bio-agent -->
Yi-Hua Huang is a researcher at the University of Hong Kong specializing in vision-language models and game-based AI benchmarking. He co-authored OmniGameArena, a unified Unreal Engine 5 benchmark that evaluates VLM game agents across diverse gameplay scenarios including Solo, PvP, and Coop regimes, addressing significant gaps in existing evaluation frameworks. His work introduces the Improvement Dynamics Curve harness, an innovative methodology that measures how VLM agents adapt and refine their performance through autonomous reflection across multiple rounds of repeated interaction with the same task. Through systematic evaluation of twelve VLM agents, he demonstrates that adaptive improvement strategies can diverge between origin-task performance and held-out variant transfer, revealing important insights about agent generalization. His research contributes to more comprehensive and realistic assessment methods for multimodal AI systems, with implications for developing increasingly effective VLM agents capable of learning from interactive experiences in complex virtual environments.
