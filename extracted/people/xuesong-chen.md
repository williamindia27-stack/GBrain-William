## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Xuesong Chen is a neurotechnology researcher at Beijing Five Seasons Medical Technology Co., Ltd. working at the intersection of brain-computer interfaces and deep learning. He develops novel representation learning approaches for electroencephalogram signals, most notably proposing a microstate tokenizer that discretizes continuous EEG data into sequences of quasi-stable brain states. His work demonstrates that microstate-based representations significantly outperform traditional time-domain and frequency-domain features across multiple clinical applications including sleep staging, emotion recognition, and motor imagery classification. Chen's microstate tokenizer exhibits universal transferability across diverse downstream tasks, addressing data scarcity challenges and showing improved scalability with increasing dataset sizes. His research advances both the interpretability and practical efficiency of EEG-based brain-computer interfaces with implications for cognitive neuroscience and clinical neuroinformatics.
