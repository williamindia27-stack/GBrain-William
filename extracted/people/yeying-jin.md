## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Yeying Jin is a researcher specializing in artificial intelligence and world modeling, focusing on how AI agents can understand and interact with complex environments. She contributes to foundational work on agentic world modeling through the development of the "levels×laws" taxonomy, which categorizes world model capabilities across three levels (Predictor, Simulator, and Evolver) and four governing-law regimes (physical, digital, social, and scientific). Her research synthesizes over 400 works spanning model-based reinforcement learning, video generation, web and GUI agents, multi-agent social simulation, and AI-driven scientific discovery, identifying critical failure modes and evaluation practices across different capability-regime pairs. Jin advances the field by proposing decision-centric evaluation principles and minimal reproducible evaluation packages that move beyond traditional next-step prediction toward world models capable of simulating and reshaping environments. Her work provides essential common language and architectural guidance for aligning diverse research communities around the challenge of building AI agents capable of sustained goal-directed interaction with their environments.
