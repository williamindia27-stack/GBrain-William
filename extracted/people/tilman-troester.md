## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Tilman Tröster is a cosmologist and computational researcher specializing in the development of methodologies to enhance scientific integrity in large-scale astronomical surveys and analyses. He contributes to addressing experimenter bias in cosmological measurements through advanced data processing techniques, as evidenced by his work on Smokescreen, a Python package that implements data-vector blinding and encryption methods to prevent unconscious tuning of analysis pipelines toward expected results. His research focuses on ensuring that theoretical models used for data concealment remain identical to those employed for inference, leveraging tools such as Firecrown likelihoods and the SACC data format to achieve robust and reproducible blinding procedures. Tröster's work emphasizes transparency and community verification in cosmological analysis, enabling wider inspection of how blinding shifts are computed both before and after unblinding in collaboration-wide pipelines. His contributions extend the practical toolkit available to the cosmological community for maintaining the statistical integrity and scientific credibility of high-stakes measurements from modern large-scale surveys.
