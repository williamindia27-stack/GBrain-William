## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
C. Danielle Leonard is a cosmological researcher at Newcastle University specializing in research integrity and methodological rigor in large-scale cosmological analyses. She contributes to collaborative efforts addressing experimenter bias in observational cosmology, particularly through the development of robust statistical techniques for data analysis pipelines. Leonard is a co-author of Smokescreen, a Python package that implements data-vector blinding and encryption methods to prevent unconscious analyst bias in cosmological parameter inference. Her work focuses on ensuring that theoretical modeling frameworks remain consistent between the blinding and inference stages of analysis, leveraging Firecrown likelihoods and standardized data formats to achieve reproducible and transparent scientific practices. Through these contributions, Leonard advances the integrity of cosmological measurements by providing practical tools that the wider community can inspect and verify before and after results are unblinded.
