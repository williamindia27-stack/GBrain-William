## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Yangqin Jiang is a researcher at the University of Hong Kong working at the intersection of artificial intelligence and financial markets. He co-develops AI-Trader, a benchmark that evaluates autonomous agents in real-time financial decision-making across U.S. stocks, A-shares, and cryptocurrencies using a fully autonomous minimal information paradigm. His research reveals critical limitations in current Large Language Models' autonomous execution capabilities, demonstrating that risk control capacity is the primary determinant of cross-market robustness and that agent performance varies significantly across differently regulated market environments. Through systematic evaluation of mainstream LLMs operating without human guidance, he exposes fundamental challenges in model generalization and autonomous risk management in high-stakes financial environments. His work advances the field by establishing the necessity for live, data-uncontaminated evaluation frameworks that accurately reflect real-world market volatility and uncertainty, moving beyond static benchmarks to assess genuine autonomous agent capabilities.
