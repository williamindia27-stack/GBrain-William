## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Idan Shenfeld is a researcher at MIT working in the intersection of reinforcement learning and language model optimization. His work focuses on improving test-time search performance through novel training approaches, particularly through Vector Policy Optimization (VPO), which trains models to produce diverse solutions by combining multi-answer generation with stochastic reward scalarizations. VPO addresses a critical limitation of standard post-training methods by enabling models to span the Pareto frontier of possible solutions, demonstrating improvements across multiple benchmarks including coding tasks, multi-hop question answering, and logic reasoning. His research demonstrates that VPO-trained models can unlock problems that conventional methods like GRPO cannot solve, even with equivalent computational budgets. Shenfeld's work contributes to a broader vision of making language models more capable exploration and exploitation agents for complex problem-solving tasks that require discovering diverse solution pathways.
