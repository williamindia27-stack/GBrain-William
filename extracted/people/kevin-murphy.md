## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Kevin Murphy is a computer scientist at the University of British Columbia whose research focuses on advancing forecasting systems through the integration of probabilistic reasoning and large language models. He developed the Bayesian Linguistic Forecaster (BLF), a binary forecasting system that achieves state-of-the-art performance on the ForecastBench benchmark by combining numerical probability estimates with natural-language evidence summaries in a semi-structured Bayesian belief state. His methodological innovations include hierarchical multi-trial aggregation using logit-space shrinkage and hierarchical calibration techniques with Platt scaling to prevent over-shrinkage of extreme predictions. The BLF system demonstrates performance comparable to human superforecasters, with a difficulty-adjusted Brier Index of 71.0, and outperforms established methods including Cassi, GPT-5, and Foresight-32B with statistical significance. Murphy's work has implications for decision-making across diverse domains including geopolitics, finance, and public health, demonstrating the potential for agentic AI systems to enhance forecasting accuracy through iterative tool use and principled probabilistic methods.
