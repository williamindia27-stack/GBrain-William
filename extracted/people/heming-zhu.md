## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Heming Zhu is a computer scientist at the Max Planck Institute for Informatics whose research focuses on neural rendering and efficient digital human representation for immersive applications. His work on MUA (Mobile Ultra-detailed Animatable Avatars) introduces a wavelet-guided multi-level spatial factorized blendshape representation that decomposes high-resolution Gaussian splat textures into manageable components, enabling the creation of photorealistic animatable avatars with dramatically reduced computational demands. Through knowledge distillation techniques, he achieves up to 2000× lower computational cost and 10× smaller model sizes while preserving visual fidelity and motion-dependent details comparable to high-quality teacher models. His approach enables real-time on-device inference of photorealistic avatars on mobile VR platforms, running at 24 FPS on Meta Quest 3 headsets and over 180 FPS on desktop systems. Zhu's research bridges the longstanding gap between avatar fidelity and computational efficiency, with significant implications for advancing immersive virtual and mixed reality experiences.
