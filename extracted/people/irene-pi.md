## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Irene Pi is a researcher at Carnegie Mellon University working in computer vision and multimodal machine learning. She contributes to advancing video-language understanding through human-AI collaboration frameworks, particularly in the CHAI project where she helps develop structured specifications for precise video description with professional video creators. Her work focuses on bridging the gap between current video-language models and the fine-grained visual understanding needed for applications like text-to-video generation, including motion dynamics, camera work, and cinematographic details. Through critique-based oversight mechanisms, she demonstrates how open-source models can be efficiently improved to match or exceed closed-source alternatives with modest expert supervision. Her research aims to enable more precise language control in video content creation and generation tasks.
