## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Isha Puri is a researcher at MIT working in machine learning and reinforcement learning for language models. She contributes to advancing test-time search performance through novel training methodologies, particularly through Vector Policy Optimization (VPO), which combines multi-answer generation with stochastic reward scalarizations to train models that produce diverse solutions spanning the Pareto frontier. Her work demonstrates that VPO-trained models outperform standard approaches across multiple challenging domains including multi-hop question answering, logic reasoning, navigation, tool use, and coding tasks, with performance gains that increase substantially as candidate budgets grow. The VPO approach addresses a critical limitation in contemporary language model post-training by enabling exploration and exploitation rather than collapsing to low-entropy response distributions. Her research vision centers on improving language model capabilities for complex problem-solving tasks that require searching over diverse solution spaces.
