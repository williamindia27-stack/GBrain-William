## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Chancharik Mitra is a computer science researcher at Carnegie Mellon University working at the intersection of multimodal learning and video understanding. His research focuses on precise video language understanding and generation, as demonstrated in his work on CHAI, a critique-based human-AI oversight framework that improves video captioning through structured specifications and model-generated pre-captions refined by human critique. He investigates how explicit preference and critique supervision can enhance both standard supervised fine-tuning and reinforcement learning methods, enabling open-source models to outperform closed-source counterparts with modest expert annotation. His work addresses critical limitations in current video-language models, particularly their difficulty with fine-grained aspects such as subject motion and camera dynamics while handling subject appearance and scene context. Through his research on precise language control for text-to-video generation, Mitra contributes to enabling professional-grade video content creation tools that can follow detailed compositional and cinematographic specifications. His broader vision encompasses developing scalable oversight frameworks that efficiently leverage human expertise to advance multimodal AI systems.
