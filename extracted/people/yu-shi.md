## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Yu Shi is a researcher at Tsinghua University specializing in financial machine learning and time series analysis. He leads the development of Kronos, a foundation model engineered specifically for financial K-line data that addresses critical limitations in existing Time Series Foundation Models through a specialized tokenization approach and autoregressive pre-training on over 12 billion records across 45 global markets. His work demonstrates substantial improvements in financial forecasting tasks, achieving a 93% boost in price series forecasting RankIC compared to leading alternatives and 9% lower error rates in volatility prediction. Beyond forecasting, Shi's research extends to synthetic financial data generation and quantitative finance applications, positioning Kronos as a unified, scalable framework for diverse financial time series tasks. His contributions represent a significant advancement in developing generalizable, robust machine learning tools that can effectively bridge the gap between foundational models and practical financial institution needs across multiple markets and temporal granularities.
