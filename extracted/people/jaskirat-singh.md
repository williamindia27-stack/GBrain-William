## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Jaskirat Singh is a researcher at the Australian National University (ANU) specializing in AI agent development and software engineering automation. He contributes to the OpenHands platform, a comprehensive framework that enables AI agents to perform complex tasks including code writing, command-line interaction, and web browsing through a modular event stream architecture and sandboxed runtime environment. His work involves the implementation and evaluation of both generalist and specialist AI agents across challenging benchmarks such as SWE-BENCH and WEBARENA, demonstrating practical capabilities in autonomous software development. Singh's research addresses critical challenges in safe AI agent interaction with computing environments, including code execution safety and multi-agent coordination mechanisms. Through contributions to the OpenHands community project, which has attracted over 2.1K contributions from 188 collaborators, his work aims to advance the development of AI systems capable of performing complex real-world tasks comparable to human developers.
