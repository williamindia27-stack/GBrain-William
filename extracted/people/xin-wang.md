## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Xin Wang is a researcher in neuroinformatics and brain-computer interfaces affiliated with Beijing Five Seasons Medical Technology Co., Ltd. His work focuses on developing novel representation learning approaches for electroencephalogram (EEG) signal analysis, particularly through the lens of brain microstates. In "Atoms Of Thought: Universal EEG Representation Learning With Microstates," Wang and his collaborators propose a microstate tokenizer that discretizes continuous EEG signals into interpretable quasi-stable patterns, demonstrating superior performance compared to traditional time-domain and frequency-domain features across sleep staging, emotion recognition, and motor imagery classification tasks. His research demonstrates that microstate representations can generalize effectively across multiple downstream tasks, addressing critical challenges of data scarcity in clinical and cognitive neuroscience applications. Wang's contributions suggest a pathway toward more scalable, interpretable, and cognitively grounded approaches to understanding brain activity through EEG analysis.
