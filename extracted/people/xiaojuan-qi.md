## Overview


<!-- bio-agent -->
Xiaojuan Qi is a researcher at The University of Hong Kong specializing in vision-language models and their evaluation in complex interactive environments. She leads work on comprehensive benchmarking frameworks for VLM game agents, as demonstrated through her development of OmniGameArena, a unified Unreal Engine 5 benchmark that spans Solo, PvP, and Coop game regimes to assess agent capabilities beyond single-player scenarios. Her research introduces methodological innovations such as the Improvement Dynamics Curve harness, which measures how VLM agents adapt and refine their performance through autonomous reflection across multiple interaction rounds. A key finding from her work reveals that VLM agents exhibit heterogeneous performance profiles with no single model dominating across diverse game types, and that agents can significantly improve from cold-start baselines through iterative skill refinement. Her research addresses critical gaps in VLM evaluation by providing realistic, multi-agent scenarios that better reflect real-world application requirements and highlighting the importance of studying both task-specific improvement and generalization to held-out variants.
