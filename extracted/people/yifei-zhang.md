## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Yifei Zhang is a researcher at the University of Hong Kong working at the intersection of artificial intelligence and financial technology. He is a key contributor to the AI-Trader benchmark, which pioneered a fully autonomous, real-time evaluation paradigm for assessing Large Language Models in live financial markets across U.S. stocks, A-shares, and cryptocurrencies. His work reveals critical limitations in current autonomous agents, demonstrating that most LLM-based trading systems exhibit poor returns and weak risk management when operating without human intervention, while also exposing significant cross-market generalization failures. Zhang's research highlights how risk control capabilities determine robustness across different market environments, with trading strategies showing notably better performance in highly liquid markets compared to policy-driven markets. His contributions establish the necessity for live, realistic evaluation of autonomous agents in high-stakes financial applications where dynamic decision-making under uncertainty is paramount.
