## Overview

Hoang H. Tran is a researcher affiliated with HCMUT (Ho Chi Minh University of Technology) specializing in artificial intelligence agents and software development. He contributes to the OpenHands platform, a comprehensive system that enables AI agents to write code, interact with command-line interfaces, and browse the web through a modular architecture featuring event stream interactions and sandboxed runtime environments. His work on OpenHands involves the development and integration of multiple specialized agents, including generalist agents and domain-specific agents for web browsing and code editing, as well as evaluation frameworks that assess agent performance across challenging tasks such as SWE-BENCH and WEBARENA. Tran's research addresses the critical challenge of building AI agents capable of complex interactions with computational environments while maintaining safety through sandboxed execution and multi-agent coordination mechanisms. His contributions to the OpenHands platform, which has attracted over 2.1K contributions from 188 community members, reflect a broader research vision of advancing generalist AI systems that can autonomously assist in software engineering, web development, and scientific research.
