Weijian Ma is a researcher specializing in artificial intelligence and world modeling, with a focus on enabling autonomous agents to understand and interact with complex environments. He contributes to the foundational taxonomy and evaluation frameworks for agentic world modeling, collaborating on comprehensive syntheses that organize over 400 works across model-based reinforcement learning, video generation, agent systems, and AI-driven scientific discovery. His work introduces the "levels×laws" framework that categorizes world modeling capabilities from basic prediction through simulation to evidence-driven environmental revision, while identifying critical failure modes across physical, digital, social, and scientific domains. Ma emphasizes decision-centric evaluation principles and minimal reproducible evaluation methodologies to advance rigorous assessment of world modeling systems. His research vision centers on bridging disparate AI communities through common conceptual language and providing architectural guidance to support agents in accomplishing sustained, goal-directed interactions with their environments.
