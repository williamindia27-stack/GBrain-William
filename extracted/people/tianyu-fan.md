## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Tianyu Fan is a researcher at the University of Hong Kong specializing in autonomous agents and their real-world applications in financial decision-making. He leads the development of AI-Trader, a benchmark for evaluating Large Language Models in live financial markets across U.S. stocks, A-shares, and cryptocurrencies, employing a fully autonomous minimal information paradigm where agents must independently search, verify, and synthesize market information in real-time. His research reveals critical limitations in current LLM agents, demonstrating that most exhibit poor returns and weak risk management in autonomous operations, with significant cross-market generalization failures and performance disparities between liquid and policy-driven market environments. Through rigorous evaluation of six mainstream LLMs across multiple trading frequencies, Fan identifies risk control capability as the primary determinant of cross-market robustness. His work addresses a fundamental gap in agent evaluation by providing realistic assessment under actual market volatility, moving beyond static benchmarks to expose the challenges autonomous systems face in dynamic, high-stakes financial environments.
