## Overview

Shihang Zhu is a computer science researcher at Carnegie Mellon University specializing in multimodal machine learning, video-language understanding, and human-AI collaboration. His work focuses on developing precise frameworks for video description and caption generation, as demonstrated in CHAI, where he contributed to designing critique-based oversight systems that combine model-generated pre-captions with human feedback to produce enhanced video annotations. Through this research, Zhu has shown that explicit preference and critique supervision can enable open-source vision-language models to outperform closed-source alternatives, while also advancing fine-grained control over motion, camera dynamics, and cinematographic elements in text-to-video generation. His contributions to structured video description specifications developed collaboratively with professional video creators highlight an emphasis on bridging the gap between technical model capabilities and practical creative applications. By advancing human-AI oversight frameworks and critique quality assessment, Zhu's research aims to enable professional-grade video content creation with precise linguistic control.
