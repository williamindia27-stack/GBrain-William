## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Jize Zhang is a researcher at the University of Oxford specializing in artificial intelligence and world modeling for autonomous agents. He contributes to foundational frameworks for understanding how AI systems can build representations of dynamic environments, as demonstrated through his work on "Agentic World Modeling," where he helped develop the levels×laws taxonomy that categorizes world modeling capabilities across three levels (Predictor, Simulator, and Evolver) and four governing-law regimes (physical, digital, social, and scientific). His research synthesizes over 400 works spanning model-based reinforcement learning, video generation, multi-agent social simulation, and AI-driven scientific discovery, providing a comprehensive taxonomy for analyzing failure modes and evaluation practices. Zhang advocates for decision-centric evaluation principles and reproducible evaluation methodologies that can advance the practical deployment of world models across diverse domains. His work aims to establish a common language across research communities and chart a path toward AI systems capable of simulating and reshaping their environments to accomplish sustained goals.
