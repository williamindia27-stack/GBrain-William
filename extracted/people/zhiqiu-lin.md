## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Zhiqiu Lin is a computer scientist at Carnegie Mellon University whose research focuses on advancing video-language understanding and generation. His work addresses fundamental limitations in how vision-language models describe dynamic visual content, developing structured specifications for video description in collaboration with professional creators. In the CHAI framework, Lin and his team introduce a critique-based human-AI oversight approach that leverages model-generated pre-captions and human feedback to produce significantly improved video annotations, demonstrating that open-source models can surpass closed-source alternatives with modest expert supervision. His research reveals that current video-language models struggle with fine-grained aspects such as subject motion and camera dynamics while excelling at appearance and scene understanding, and shows that explicit preference and critique supervision substantially enhances both supervised fine-tuning and reinforcement learning methods. Through this work, Lin enables precise language control in text-to-video generation, supporting professional-grade applications where detailed prompts of hundreds of words can guide visual output across motion, cinematography, and composition dimensions. His research vision centers on creating scalable frameworks that combine human expertise with model capabilities to unlock more effective and efficient multimodal content creation.
