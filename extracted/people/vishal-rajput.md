## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Vishal Rajput is a machine learning researcher at KU Leuven whose work focuses on representation learning and robustness to distribution shift. His research develops geometric theories to understand and mitigate deployment drift, particularly through the Matching Principle, which provides a unified framework for understanding robustness techniques including adversarial training, CORAL, and Jacobian-based penalties. He introduces novel methods for estimating covariance matrices of label-preserving nuisances and regularizing encoder Jacobians through trace penalties, alongside the Trajectory Deviation Index as a diagnostic tool for measuring embedding sensitivity. His theoretical contributions establish optimality conditions in linear-Gaussian models and prove necessity results for quadratic Jacobian penalties to achieve zero drift. Rajput's work addresses a critical gap in making machine learning models more reliable in real-world applications where nuisance variables and deployment drift present persistent challenges to model performance.
