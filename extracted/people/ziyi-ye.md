## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Ziyi Ye is a researcher at Fudan University working in the intersection of neuroscience and deep learning, with a focus on electroencephalogram signal analysis and brain-computer interfaces. In "Atoms of Thought: Universal EEG Representation Learning with Microstates," Ye contributes to a novel microstate tokenization approach that converts continuous EEG signals into discrete, interpretable patterns using k-means clustering. This methodology demonstrates superior performance across multiple downstream tasks including sleep staging, emotion recognition, and motor imagery classification compared to traditional time-domain and frequency-domain feature extraction methods. The work establishes that microstate representations exhibit improved scalability and generalizability, allowing tokenizers trained on one task to effectively transfer to others while addressing the persistent challenge of limited EEG data availability. Ye's research points toward transformative applications in clinical neuroscience and cognitive research by providing more interpretable and computationally efficient representations of brain activity that can link surface-level electrical patterns to fundamental cognitive and affective processes.
