## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Ziqi Huang is a researcher specializing in world modeling and artificial intelligence systems designed to interact with and understand complex environments. As a co-author of "Agentic World Modeling," Huang contributed to synthesizing over 400 works and summarizing more than 100 representative systems spanning model-based reinforcement learning, video generation, web and GUI agents, multi-agent social simulation, and AI-driven scientific discovery. The research introduces a novel "levels×laws" taxonomy that categorizes world modeling capabilities across three levels (Predictor, Simulator, and Evolver) and four governing-law regimes (physical, digital, social, and scientific), establishing a common language across disparate research communities. Huang's work identifies critical failure modes in world models and proposes decision-centric evaluation principles with a minimal reproducible evaluation package to advance the field. The broader vision of this research is to chart a path from passive prediction toward world models that can actively simulate and reshape environments, enabling agents to accomplish sustained, goal-directed interaction with complex systems.
