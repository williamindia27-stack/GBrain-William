## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Frank F. Xu is a computer scientist based at Carnegie Mellon University whose research focuses on the development of AI agents capable of complex interaction with computing environments. He contributes to foundational work on agent platforms and architectures, notably serving as a core team member on OpenHands, a comprehensive platform that enables AI agents to write code, interact with command-line interfaces, and browse the web. His work emphasizes modular design principles, including event stream architectures and sandboxed runtime environments that allow safe and coordinated multi-agent execution. Through OpenHands, Xu has helped establish evaluation frameworks for assessing AI agents across challenging real-world tasks such as software engineering benchmarks and web-based problem solving. His research aims to advance the development of generalist and specialist AI agents that can operate with human-like flexibility in software development and beyond, contributing to a community-driven platform that has garnered over 2,100 contributions from hundreds of collaborators.
