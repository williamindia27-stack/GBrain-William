## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Nora Elisa Chisari is a cosmological researcher at Utrecht University specializing in experimental design and data analysis integrity in large-scale surveys. She collaborates on critical methodological tools that address systematic challenges in modern cosmology, including the development of Smokescreen, a Python package that implements data-vector blinding and encryption to prevent experimenter bias in cosmological analyses. Her work focuses on ensuring scientific reproducibility by creating transparent, standardized procedures for concealing data during analysis, utilizing frameworks such as Firecrown likelihoods and the SACC format to maintain consistency between blinding and inference models. Chisari's contributions emphasize making concealment procedures verifiable and inspectable by the broader research community, both prior to and following unblinding of results. Her research vision centers on safeguarding the integrity of cosmological measurements through practical, collaborative tools that can be integrated into large collaboration-wide analysis pipelines.
