## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Xiangru Tang is a researcher at Yale University working in artificial intelligence and software engineering. He contributes to the development of autonomous AI agents capable of complex interactions with computing environments, as demonstrated through his work on the OpenHands platform. His research focuses on building modular architectures that enable AI agents to write code, interact with command lines, and browse the web through event stream-based interaction patterns and sandboxed runtime environments. Tang's work on OpenHands encompasses the design of multi-agent delegation systems and evaluation frameworks that assess agent performance across challenging tasks including software engineering benchmarks like SWE-BENCH and web interaction tasks like WEBARENA. His research vision centers on advancing generalist and specialist AI agents that can perform complex software development tasks safely and effectively, contributing to the broader goal of creating AI systems that interact with the world in ways comparable to human developers.
