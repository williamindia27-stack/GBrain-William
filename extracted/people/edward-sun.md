## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Edward Sun is a researcher at the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA) specializing in the intersection of large language models and financial technology. He contributes to the development of multi-agent systems for financial applications, notably co-authoring the TradingAgents framework, which leverages LLM-powered agents in specialized roles such as fundamental analysts, sentiment analysts, and technical analysts to simulate collaborative trading environments. His work demonstrates that hybrid approaches combining structured outputs with natural language dialogue can improve key trading metrics including cumulative returns, Sharpe ratio, and maximum drawdown compared to baseline models. Sun's research addresses the limitations of traditional single-agent and multi-agent systems in algorithmic trading by incorporating the domain-specific reasoning capabilities of fine-tuned financial language models. His contributions advance the broader goal of enhancing explainability and transparency in AI-driven financial decision-making through collaborative multi-agent architectures.
