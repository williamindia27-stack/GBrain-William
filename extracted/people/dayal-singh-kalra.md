## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Dayal Singh Kalra is a machine learning researcher at the University of Maryland, College Park focused on optimizing the training efficiency of large language models. His research investigates hyperparameter transfer, specifically developing frameworks to quantify how optimal optimization hyperparameters can be extrapolated from small to large model scales. Kalra's work identifies the embedding layer learning rate as the primary determinant of successful hyperparameter transfer, demonstrating that Maximal Update Parameterization dramatically improves transfer robustness compared to standard parameterization approaches. His research introduces three key metrics to evaluate hyperparameter transfer quality: the scaling law fit error, robustness to extrapolation errors, and asymptotic loss degradation. Kalra's contributions have practical significance for reducing expensive hyperparameter search costs in large-scale language model training and informing more effective tuning strategies across neural network architectures.
