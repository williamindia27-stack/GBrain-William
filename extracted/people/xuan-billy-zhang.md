## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Xuan Billy Zhang is a researcher at the National University of Singapore working in artificial intelligence and agent systems, with a focus on world modeling for autonomous agents. He co-authored "Agentic World Modeling," a comprehensive synthesis of over 400 works that introduces a "levels×laws" taxonomy organizing world modeling capabilities across three levels (Predictor, Simulator, Evolver) and four governing-law regimes (physical, digital, social, and scientific). His work analyzes failure modes and evaluation practices in diverse applications including model-based reinforcement learning, video generation, web and GUI agents, multi-agent social simulation, and AI-driven scientific discovery. Zhang contributes methodologically through the development of decision-centric evaluation principles and a minimal reproducible evaluation package designed to standardize assessment practices across world modeling systems. His research aims to provide a unifying framework that advances the field from passive next-step prediction toward world models capable of simulating and reshaping complex environments, which is essential for agents to accomplish sustained goals through extended interaction.
