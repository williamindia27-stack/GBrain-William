## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Hewei Wang is a computer science researcher at Carnegie Mellon University specializing in video-language understanding and multimodal machine learning. He contributes to the development of precise video language models that address the limitations of current systems in describing dynamic visual content, as demonstrated in his work on the CHAI framework. His research focuses on human-AI oversight mechanisms that leverage model-generated captions and expert critiques to produce significantly improved video descriptions with fine-grained control over motion, camera dynamics, and cinematography. Wang's work demonstrates that explicit preference and critique supervision can enable open-source models to outperform closed-source alternatives, with particular emphasis on ensuring critique quality through precision, recall, and constructiveness. His research has direct applications to professional-grade text-to-video generation systems and advancing the capabilities of video content creation tools. Through this work, Wang contributes to bridging the gap between human creative intent and machine-generated visual content through more precise language understanding.
