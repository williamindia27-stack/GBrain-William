## Overview


<!-- bio-agent -->
Weixian Xu is a researcher at Shanghai Jiao Tong University working in machine learning and natural language processing, with a focus on adaptive learning systems for foundation models. His research addresses critical challenges in deploying language models to real-world environments through innovative prompt learning strategies. In Eevee, a test-time prompt learning framework co-authored with colleagues from Princeton University, Xu develops a router-prompt co-evolution strategy that enables self-improving agents to handle heterogeneous input streams from multiple datasets without experiencing cross-dataset interference. The framework achieves substantial performance improvements, surpassing state-of-the-art methods by up to 48.2% and maintaining cumulative retention gains of +41.53 in incremental multi-benchmark settings where competing approaches show significant performance degradation. His work demonstrates how lightweight adaptation mechanisms can refine foundation model behavior after deployment, enabling practical solutions for task streams with diverse and changing distributions. Xu's research vision centers on developing robust, scalable approaches for autonomous model improvement in dynamic real-world environments.
