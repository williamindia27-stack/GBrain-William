## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Pulkit Agrawal is a computer science researcher at MIT who specializes in reinforcement learning and language model training. His work focuses on improving test-time search performance through novel training approaches that encourage model diversity, as exemplified in his development of Vector Policy Optimization (VPO), which trains language models to produce diverse solutions by combining multi-answer generation with stochastic reward scalarizations. VPO addresses a critical limitation of standard post-training methods that produce low-entropy response distributions by training models to anticipate diverse reward functions and span the Pareto frontier of possible solutions. His experimental results demonstrate that VPO-trained models outperform strong baselines on multi-hop question answering, logic reasoning, navigation, tool use, and coding tasks, while unlocking problems that conventional methods cannot solve. Agrawal's research vision centers on bridging the gap between model training and practical search procedures by developing techniques that enable exploration and exploitation in downstream applications.
