## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Yuxuan Chen is a researcher at the University of Hong Kong specializing in autonomous agents and their real-world applications in financial decision-making. He is a core contributor to the AI-Trader benchmark, which evaluates Large Language Models as autonomous trading agents across multiple global markets including U.S. stocks, A-shares, and cryptocurrencies in live, real-time conditions. His research reveals critical limitations in current LLM agents' autonomous execution capabilities, risk management, and cross-market generalization, demonstrating that risk control capacity is the primary determinant of robustness across different trading environments. Through the development of a minimal information paradigm requiring agents to independently search, verify, and synthesize live market data without human intervention, Chen's work addresses the gap between static evaluation benchmarks and the dynamic demands of actual financial markets. His research emphasizes the necessity for live evaluation frameworks in assessing autonomous agent capabilities for real-world, high-stakes applications.
