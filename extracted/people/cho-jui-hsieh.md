## Overview


<!-- bio-agent -->
Cho-Jui Hsieh is a computer scientist at the University of California, Los Angeles specializing in machine learning optimization and large language model training. His research addresses fundamental challenges in supervised fine-tuning by proposing the Q-target framework, which reinterprets SFT as a target distribution design problem that decomposes supervision into explicit choices about token probability assignment and alternative token allocation. Through this theoretical lens, he demonstrates how the Q-target framework unifies diverse existing SFT variants and introduces TARGET-SFT, a method that consistently outperforms across multiple dataset-model settings by better balancing dataset imitation, prior preservation, and alternative supervision. His work exposes a general design space for SFT objectives that moves beyond standard token prediction by strategically allocating probability mass to non-observed tokens based on model priors and dataset characteristics. Hsieh's research contributes to more efficient and effective post-training of large language models by providing principled design principles that improve both theoretical understanding and practical performance of fine-tuning procedures.
