## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Jonathan Blazek is a cosmological researcher at Northeastern University who specializes in addressing systematic biases and methodological challenges in large-scale cosmological analyses. He is a core contributor to Smokescreen, a Python package that implements data-vector blinding and encryption methods to prevent experimenter bias in cosmological experiments using Firecrown likelihoods and the SACC data format. His work focuses on designing practical solutions that ensure the scientific integrity of cosmological measurements by applying cosmology-dependent shifts to observed data vectors while preserving their statistical properties. Blazek's contributions to this collaborative effort demonstrate expertise in both the theoretical foundations of cosmological inference and the software engineering practices necessary for reproducible, transparent analysis pipelines. His research aims to establish standardized methodologies for bias mitigation that can be integrated across wide collaborations, thereby advancing the reliability of modern cosmological experiments.
