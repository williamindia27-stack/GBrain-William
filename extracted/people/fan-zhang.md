## Overview


<!-- bio-agent -->
Fan Zhang is a researcher at The Chinese University of Hong Kong whose work focuses on evaluating and improving vision-language model agents in complex interactive environments. He contributes to the development of comprehensive benchmarking frameworks for VLM game agents, as demonstrated in OmniGameArena, a unified Unreal Engine 5 benchmark that extends evaluation beyond single-agent scenarios to include PvP and cooperative gameplay modes. His research introduces methodological innovations such as the Improvement Dynamics Curve harness, which measures how VLM agents adapt and refine their performance through autonomous reflection across multiple interaction rounds. Through systematic evaluation of heterogeneous agents ranging from commercial to open-weight VLMs, he reveals that agent performance trajectories are non-monotonic and that in-task improvement does not necessarily transfer to held-out variants. His work addresses a critical gap in VLM evaluation by providing realistic assessment frameworks that capture agent learning dynamics rather than static cold-start performance, with implications for developing more robust multimodal agents in interactive real-world applications.
