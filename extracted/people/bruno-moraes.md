## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Bruno Moraes is a cosmological researcher affiliated with CBPF - Centro Brasileiro de Pesquisas Físicas who specializes in addressing methodological challenges in large-scale cosmological analyses. He contributes to efforts to combat experimenter bias in cosmology through work on data-vector blinding techniques and reproducible analysis pipelines. His research involves implementing robust encryption and blinding methods for cosmological data that preserve statistical properties while concealing true cosmological signals, working with Firecrown likelihoods and the SACC data format to ensure consistency between blinding and inference procedures. Moraes collaborates on the Smokescreen Python package, which provides a standardized, transparent, and verifiable implementation of data-vector blinding methodology with high code coverage standards. Through these contributions to scientific integrity and reproducibility in cosmology, he helps establish rigorous practices that allow the wider research community to inspect and validate how concealment procedures are computed before and after unblinding.
