## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Isaac Li is a computer scientist at Carnegie Mellon University specializing in multimodal learning and video understanding. He contributes to research on precise video language understanding through human-AI collaboration frameworks, as demonstrated in CHAI, where he helps develop methods for improving video captioning through critique-based oversight. His work addresses fundamental limitations in current video-language models, particularly their struggles with fine-grained aspects such as subject motion and camera dynamics. Through the CHAI framework, Li's research shows that explicit preference and critique supervision can enable open-source models to outperform closed-source alternatives with modest expert guidance. His research vision centers on enabling professional-grade content creation tools through improved video-language understanding and precise natural language control over video generation systems.
