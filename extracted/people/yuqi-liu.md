## Overview


<!-- bio-agent -->
Yuqi Liu is a researcher at the Chinese University of Hong Kong working at the intersection of vision-language models and interactive game environments. He contributes to the development of comprehensive benchmarking frameworks for evaluating multimodal AI agents, as demonstrated through his work on OmniGameArena, a unified benchmark that spans solo, competitive, and cooperative gameplay modes across twelve newly constructed Unreal Engine 5 games. His research introduces methodological innovations such as the Improvement Dynamics Curve harness, which enables autonomous reflection and skill refinement across multiple interaction rounds, revealing that VLM agents can meaningfully adapt their performance through repeated engagement with tasks. Liu's work emphasizes the critical distinction between cold-start performance and improvement trajectories, demonstrating that peak agent capability often emerges mid-training rather than at convergence, and that task-specific improvement may not transfer reliably to held-out variants. His research vision centers on developing more rigorous and realistic evaluation frameworks that capture the dynamic adaptation capabilities of vision-language models in complex, interactive environments relevant to real-world applications.
