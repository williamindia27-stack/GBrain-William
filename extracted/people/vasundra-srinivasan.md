## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Vasundra Srinivasan is a systems researcher at Stanford School of Engineering who focuses on the engineering foundations of production large language model agents. She develops methodology for selecting and composing runtime architecture patterns by centering analysis on the stochastic-deterministic boundary (SDB), the critical interface where LLM outputs become system actions, and proposes a four-part contract specification for this boundary alongside a catalog of six composable patterns. Her empirical validation demonstrates that 71% of published LLM agent failures localize to SDB weaknesses and that architectural momentum dominates model selection as the primary lever for long-run reliability, findings supported by analysis of widely-used open-source frameworks and end-to-end application across conversational, autonomous, and long-horizon runtime classes. Srinivasan's work identifies and makes legible specific failure modes like replay divergence, where model version changes cause divergent behavior in deterministic event log consumers, advancing the field's ability to anticipate and prevent production failures. Her research vision positions architectural design patterns as the foundation for reliable AI systems deployment, shifting focus from isolated model improvements to the systemic composition of stochastic and deterministic components.
