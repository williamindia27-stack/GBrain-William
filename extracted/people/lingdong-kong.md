## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Lingdong Kong is a researcher at the National University of Singapore working in the domain of artificial intelligence, particularly focused on world modeling and agent-based systems. He is a key contributor to foundational work on agentic world modeling, co-authoring a comprehensive synthesis of over 400 works that provides a common language for aligning diverse research communities. His research introduces the "levels×laws" taxonomy, a novel framework that categorizes world modeling capabilities across three levels (Predictor, Simulator, and Evolver) and four governing-law regimes (physical, digital, social, and scientific). Through this taxonomy, Kong and collaborators analyze failure modes and evaluation practices across different capability-regime pairs, proposing decision-centric evaluation principles essential for advancing the field. His work establishes architectural guidance and identifies open problems in transforming world models from passive next-step predictors into systems capable of simulating and reshaping environments. Kong's research vision centers on enabling AI agents to accomplish sustained interaction goals through improved environment modeling and evaluation methodologies.
