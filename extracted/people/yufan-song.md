## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Yufan Song is a computer science researcher at Carnegie Mellon University who specializes in artificial intelligence agents and software development automation. He is a core contributor to the OpenHands platform, a comprehensive system that enables AI agents to write code, interact with command-line interfaces, and browse the web in complex ways similar to human developers. His work on OpenHands involves designing modular architecture components including event stream systems, sandboxed runtime environments, and multi-agent delegation mechanisms for coordinating specialized AI systems. The platform he helped develop includes an agent hub with over 10 implemented agents and supports evaluation across challenging benchmarks such as SWE-BENCH and WEBARENA, demonstrating practical applications in software engineering and web development. Through his contributions to this community-driven project that has attracted over 2,100 contributions from 188 developers, Song is advancing the capability of AI systems to interact with and manipulate complex digital environments safely and effectively.
