## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Yijia Xiao is a researcher at the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA) working at the intersection of large language models, multi-agent systems, and financial technology. His research focuses on developing collaborative AI frameworks for complex financial domains, as exemplified by the TradingAgents framework, which simulates professional trading environments by deploying specialized LLM-powered agents in roles such as fundamental analysts, sentiment analysts, and technical analysts. This work demonstrates that hybrid approaches combining structured outputs with natural language dialogue can achieve notable improvements in trading performance metrics including cumulative returns, Sharpe ratio, and maximum drawdown compared to baseline models. Xiao's research emphasizes the practical significance of explainability and transparency in AI-driven trading decisions, addressing key limitations of traditional algorithmic systems. His broader research vision encompasses advancing domain-adapted language models for finance and extending multi-agent collaborative frameworks beyond trading to other complex decision-making environments.
