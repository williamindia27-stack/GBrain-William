## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Omar Khattab is a computer scientist at MIT whose research focuses on improving language model performance through advanced training and inference techniques. He contributes to reinforcement learning methods for language models, including work on Vector Policy Optimization (VPO), which trains models to produce diverse solutions across the Pareto frontier by combining multi-answer generation with stochastic reward scalarizations. His research demonstrates that diversity-aware training significantly improves test-time search performance, unlocking problems unsolvable by standard post-training approaches across coding, reasoning, and multi-hop question answering tasks. Khattab's work addresses the fundamental challenge that standard methods produce low-entropy response distributions that hinder exploration during search procedures. His research vision centers on enabling language models to better balance exploration and exploitation through principled training approaches that anticipate multiple reward functions and diverse solution spaces.
