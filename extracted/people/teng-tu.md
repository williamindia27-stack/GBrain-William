## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Teng Tu is a researcher at the National University of Singapore working on artificial intelligence systems and world modeling. Tu contributes to the foundational work on agentic world modeling, a comprehensive synthesis of over 400 works that establishes a common language across communities studying how AI agents learn environment dynamics. His work introduces the "levels×laws" taxonomy, which categorizes world modeling capabilities into three levels (Predictor, Simulator, Evolver) across four governing-law regimes (physical, digital, social, and scientific), providing a systematic framework for understanding what constraints different types of world models must satisfy. Tu's research identifies critical failure modes and proposes decision-centric evaluation principles alongside a minimal reproducible evaluation package to advance the field. His broader vision aims to chart a path from passive next-step prediction toward world models that can actively simulate and reshape environments, which is essential for agents to accomplish complex goals through sustained interaction.
