## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Chao Huang is a researcher at the University of Hong Kong whose work focuses on evaluating autonomous agents in financial decision-making environments. His research investigates the performance limitations of Large Language Models operating in real-time financial markets through the AI-Trader benchmark, a novel evaluation platform that tests agents across U.S. stocks, A-shares, and cryptocurrency markets under fully autonomous conditions. Through this work, Huang and colleagues demonstrate that most LLM agents exhibit poor returns and weak risk management capabilities, with significant cross-market generalization failures when operating without human intervention. His findings reveal that risk control capability is the primary determinant of cross-market robustness and that trading performance varies substantially depending on market liquidity and regulatory structures. Huang's research addresses a critical gap in agent evaluation by providing realistic assessment methodologies for autonomous financial systems operating under actual market volatility and uncertainty, establishing essential benchmarks for developing more reliable autonomous trading agents.
