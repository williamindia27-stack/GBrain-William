## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Mingchen Zhuge is a researcher at KAUST whose work focuses on developing practical platforms and architectures for autonomous AI agents capable of complex real-world interaction. He is a core contributor to OpenHands, a comprehensive platform that enables AI agents to write code, interact with command-line interfaces, and browse the web through a modular event stream architecture and sandboxed runtime environments. His contributions to OpenHands include helping design systems that support multi-agent delegation and evaluation frameworks capable of assessing agent performance across challenging tasks such as software engineering benchmarks and web interaction scenarios. Zhuge's work emphasizes the importance of community-driven development, as evidenced by OpenHands' growth to over 2.1K contributions from 188 collaborators and its implementation of both generalist and specialist agents. His research addresses fundamental challenges in creating AI agents that can safely interact with complex environments while maintaining practical applicability to software development and scientific research domains.
