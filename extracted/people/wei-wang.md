## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Wei Wang is a researcher at the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA) specializing in the intersection of large language models and financial trading systems. He contributes to the TradingAgents framework, which leverages LLM-powered agents in specialized roles such as fundamental analysts, sentiment analysts, and technical analysts to simulate collaborative trading environments inspired by professional trading firms. His work demonstrates notable improvements in key financial metrics including cumulative returns, Sharpe ratio, and maximum drawdown by combining structured outputs with natural language dialogue for multi-agent decision-making. Wang's research addresses fundamental limitations in single-agent and traditional multi-agent trading systems by creating a hybrid approach that enhances both the precision and explainability of trading decisions. His contributions highlight the potential for domain-adapted language models to improve trading performance while maintaining transparency in algorithmic financial decision-making.
