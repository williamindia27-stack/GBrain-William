## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Xingyao Wang is a researcher in artificial intelligence and software engineering at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign who specializes in developing autonomous AI agents capable of complex real-world interaction. He leads the development of OpenHands, a comprehensive platform that enables AI agents to write code, interact with command-line interfaces, and browse the web through a modular event stream architecture and sandboxed runtime environment. His work implements multi-agent delegation systems and evaluation frameworks that assess agent performance across challenging tasks including software engineering benchmarks like SWE-BENCH and web interaction environments like WEBARENA. OpenHands has achieved significant community adoption with over 2,100 contributions from 188 contributors and includes an agent hub featuring both generalist and specialist agents for diverse task categories. Wang's research addresses fundamental challenges in creating AI systems that can safely interact with complex environments while coordinating multiple specialized agents, with implications for advancing autonomous software development and scientific research automation.
