## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Senqiao Yang is a researcher specializing in artificial intelligence and world modeling for autonomous agents. Yang contributed to the comprehensive "Agentic World Modeling" study that synthesized over 400 works and analyzed more than 100 representative systems across domains including model-based reinforcement learning, video generation, web and GUI agents, multi-agent social simulation, and AI-driven scientific discovery. The research introduced a novel "levels×laws" taxonomy that defines three capability levels—L1 Predictor, L2 Simulator, and L3 Evolver—organized across four governing-law regimes including physical, digital, social, and scientific domains. Yang's work also established decision-centric evaluation principles and a minimal reproducible evaluation package designed to improve assessment practices across different level-regime pairs in world modeling systems. Through this foundational framework, Yang's research provides critical guidance for advancing agentic systems that can reliably simulate and reshape environments to accomplish complex, sustained goals.
