## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Meng Chu is a researcher at Hong Kong University of Science and Technology specializing in artificial intelligence systems and world modeling for autonomous agents. His work addresses a fundamental challenge in AI: enabling agents to understand and predict environment dynamics through sustained interaction. In his comprehensive synthesis "Agentic World Modeling," he establishes a "levels×laws" taxonomy that distinguishes between three capability levels—Predictors, Simulators, and Evolvers—across four governing-law regimes including physical, digital, social, and scientific domains. His research integrates insights from over 400 works spanning model-based reinforcement learning, video generation, web agents, multi-agent simulation, and AI-driven scientific discovery while proposing decision-centric evaluation principles to assess world modeling systems. Through this systematic framework and minimal reproducible evaluation package, Chu aims to advance the field by providing common language across communities and charting a path toward world models capable of both simulating and reshaping their environments.
