## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Siyuan Cen is a computer scientist at Carnegie Mellon University working on multimodal learning and video-language understanding. His research focuses on developing frameworks for precise video language understanding, as demonstrated in his work on CHAI, a critique-based human-AI oversight framework designed to improve video captioning through structured specifications and model-generated pre-captions refined by human feedback. Through this approach, he has demonstrated that explicit preference and critique supervision can enable open-source vision-language models to outperform closed-source alternatives, even with modest expert annotation. His work addresses critical limitations in current video-language models' ability to capture fine-grained aspects such as subject motion, camera dynamics, and cinematography. The research has significant implications for professional-grade text-to-video generation, enabling more precise language control over visual content creation with detailed multi-word prompts. Cen's broader research vision centers on bridging the gap between human expertise and AI capabilities through efficient, scalable oversight frameworks that enhance multimodal understanding and generation.
