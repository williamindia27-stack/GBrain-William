## Overview


<!-- bio-agent -->
Yitang Li is a researcher at Tsinghua University working in computer science with a focus on vision-language models and game-based benchmarking. He contributes to the development of comprehensive evaluation frameworks for VLM game agents, as demonstrated through his work on OmniGameArena, a unified benchmark spanning twelve Unreal Engine 5 games across Solo, PvP, and Coop play regimes. His research involves designing novel assessment methodologies such as the Improvement Dynamics Curve harness, which measures how VLM agents adapt and refine their performance through autonomous reflection and repeated interaction with tasks. Through empirical evaluation of multiple agent types—including commercial VLMs, open-weight models, and specialized policies—he provides evidence that peak agent performance often occurs mid-trajectory rather than at convergence, revealing important insights about adaptation dynamics. His work addresses critical gaps in existing game benchmarks by enabling more realistic and comprehensive evaluation of vision-language agents in interactive environments.
