## Overview


<!-- bio-agent -->
Yuanhao Ban is a researcher at the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA) specializing in large language model training and post-training optimization. His work focuses on supervised fine-tuning (SFT) methodology, where he has developed the Q-target framework that reinterprets SFT as a target distribution design problem. The framework decomposes SFT supervision into two explicit choices: the strength of reliance on observed tokens and the allocation of probability mass over alternative tokens, unifying many existing SFT variants under a common theoretical lens. Building on this framework, Ban and collaborators introduced TARGET-SFT, a method that consistently outperforms across diverse dataset-model settings by balancing dataset imitation, prior preservation, and alternative supervision. His research advances the fundamental design principles for fine-tuning large language models, with practical implications for improving model performance and training efficiency in post-training stages.
