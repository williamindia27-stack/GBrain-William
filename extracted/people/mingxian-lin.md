## Overview


<!-- bio-agent -->
Mingxian Lin is a researcher at the University of Hong Kong specializing in vision-language models and their applications to interactive game environments. He leads the development of OmniGameArena, a unified benchmark that evaluates VLM game agents across twelve Unreal Engine 5 games spanning solo, player-versus-player, and cooperative gameplay modes. His research introduces the Improvement Dynamics Curve harness, an autonomous reflection mechanism that enables VLM agents to refine their performance through multiple rounds of interaction with the same task, revealing that peak performance often emerges mid-trajectory rather than at convergence. The work demonstrates that heterogeneous agents including commercial VLMs, open-weight models, and specialized game policies exhibit varying capabilities across different game types, with no single agent dominating the comprehensive benchmark. Lin's research addresses critical gaps in VLM evaluation by providing realistic, dynamic assessment environments that capture adaptation and generalization beyond cold-start performance. His contributions establish foundational benchmarking methodologies for developing more robust and capable multimodal agents in complex interactive settings.
