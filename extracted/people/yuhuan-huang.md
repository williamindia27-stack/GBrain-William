## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Yuhan Huang is a researcher in computer science at Carnegie Mellon University specializing in video-language understanding and multimodal learning. His work on the CHAI framework addresses critical limitations in how video-language models describe dynamic visual content by developing structured specifications for video description in collaboration with professional video creators. He has demonstrated that critique-based human-AI oversight can significantly enhance video captioning, enabling open-source models to outperform closed-source alternatives through strategic expert supervision and preference-based fine-tuning. His research reveals that while current models excel at capturing subject appearance and scene context, they struggle with fine-grained aspects like motion dynamics and camera movement, and that critique quality directly determines post-training success. Through this work, Huang contributes to enabling precise language control in professional-grade text-to-video generation, with applications supporting detailed cinematographic specification in creative content production.
