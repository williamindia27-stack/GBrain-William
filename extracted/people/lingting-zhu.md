## Overview


<!-- bio-agent -->
Lingting Zhu is a researcher at LIGHTSPEED working in the intersection of vision-language models and game-based AI evaluation. He contributed to the development of OmniGameArena, a unified benchmark for VLM game agents built on Unreal Engine 5 that spans Solo, PvP, and Coop gameplay regimes, addressing critical gaps in existing evaluation frameworks for multimodal AI systems. His work involves designing and implementing the Improvement Dynamics Curve harness, which measures how VLM agents adapt and refine their performance through autonomous reflection across multiple rounds of interaction with the same task. Through systematic evaluation of twelve VLM agents across diverse game environments, Zhu's research demonstrates that agent improvement trajectories are complex and non-monotonic, with peak performance often occurring at intermediate stages rather than final rounds. His contributions establish methodological foundations for more comprehensive and realistic assessment of vision-language models in dynamic, interactive environments, with implications for developing more robust and adaptable AI agents in real-world applications.
