## Overview


<!-- bio-agent -->
Yihang Chen is a researcher at UCLA whose work focuses on improving the post-training and fine-tuning of large language models through novel theoretical frameworks. Chen contributes to foundational research on supervised fine-tuning by proposing the Q-target framework, which reinterprets SFT as a target distribution design problem that decomposes supervision into explicit choices about token reliance and probability mass allocation over alternatives. Through the development of TARGET-SFT, Chen demonstrates consistent empirical improvements across diverse dataset-model settings while providing a unifying lens that explains existing SFT variants as implicit choices within a broader design space balancing dataset imitation, prior preservation, and alternative supervision. Chen's work exposes fundamental design principles for SFT training objectives, offering practical methods to address limitations in standard approaches caused by noisy or non-unique observed tokens. His research contributes to making large language model fine-tuning more efficient and effective, with implications for the broader field of deep learning optimization and model adaptation.
