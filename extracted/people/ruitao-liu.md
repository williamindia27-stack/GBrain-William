## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Ruitao Liu is a researcher at Tsinghua University specializing in electroencephalogram (EEG) analysis and neural signal representation learning. His work focuses on developing novel approaches to extract meaningful patterns from brain activity data, particularly through the lens of EEG microstates—quasi-stable discrete patterns of scalp electrical potential. In "Atoms of Thought: Universal EEG Representation Learning with Microstates," Liu and collaborators propose a microstate tokenizer using k-means clustering that transforms continuous EEG signals into interpretable discrete sequences, demonstrating superior performance across sleep staging, emotion recognition, and motor imagery classification tasks compared to traditional time-domain and frequency-domain features. His research reveals that microstate representations exhibit strong generalization capabilities across different downstream tasks and improved scalability with larger datasets, addressing significant challenges in EEG-based classification. Liu's work bridges computational neuroscience and practical brain-computer interface applications, advancing both the interpretability of neural representations and the efficiency of EEG analysis systems.
