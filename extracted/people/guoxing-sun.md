## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Guoxing Sun is a computer scientist at the Max Planck Institute for Informatics specializing in neural rendering and digital human synthesis. His work focuses on creating photorealistic, animatable avatars that balance visual fidelity with computational efficiency, as demonstrated in the MUA project which introduces a wavelet-guided multi-level spatial factorized blendshape representation for compact avatar modeling. His research achieves significant practical breakthroughs, including up to 2000× reduction in computational cost and 10× smaller model sizes compared to high-fidelity teacher models while preserving detailed motion dynamics and appearance. Sun's methods enable real-time on-device inference of sophisticated avatars on mobile platforms like the Meta Quest 3 headset, achieving 24 FPS on mobile and over 180 FPS on desktop systems. His contributions advance the deployment of immersive digital humans in virtual and mixed reality applications, making high-quality avatar experiences accessible on consumer hardware.
