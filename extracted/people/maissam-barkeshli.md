## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Maissam Barkeshli is a computer scientist at the University of Maryland, College Park whose research focuses on optimizing the training efficiency of large language models through principled investigation of hyperparameter transfer. His work develops quantitative frameworks to measure hyperparameter transfer quality across model scales, identifying the embedding layer learning rate as a critical factor in achieving transferable optimization settings. Barkeshli examines the relationship between different neural network parameterizations, particularly Maximal Update Parameterization and standard parameterization, to understand how architectural choices impact hyperparameter robustness and scaling law behavior. His research introduces multiple metrics for evaluating hyperparameter transfer including scaling law fit quality, extrapolation robustness, and asymptotic loss degradation, providing concrete tools for practitioners. Through this work, Barkeshli contributes to reducing the computational burden of large language model training by enabling more efficient hyperparameter search strategies that generalize from smaller to larger model scales.
