## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
James Lucas is a machine learning researcher at NVIDIA who specializes in computational efficiency and variance reduction in deep learning systems. His work focuses on reducing the computational burden of downstream tasks that leverage pretrained diffusion models as teachers, developing methods that substantially lower Monte Carlo estimator variance without introducing bias. In his research on variance reduction for expectations with diffusion teachers, Lucas contributed to the CARV method, a hierarchical Monte Carlo estimator that combines timestep importance sampling and stratified-inverse-CDF construction to achieve 2-3× effective compute multipliers in applications like text-to-3D distillation. His approach is grounded in a compute-aware variance-accounting framework that informs practical estimator design choices, demonstrating that order-of-magnitude gradient variance reductions are achievable in single-step distillation scenarios. Lucas's research carries significant practical implications for making diffusion-based systems more scalable and cost-effective, with potential savings estimated in the six to seven figure range at laboratory scale.
