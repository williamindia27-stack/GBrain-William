## Overview


<!-- bio-agent -->
Tong Xie is a machine learning researcher at the University of California, Los Angeles who specializes in the optimization and design principles underlying large language model post-training. His work focuses on supervised fine-tuning, a critical stage in LLM development, where he has developed novel theoretical frameworks to address inherent limitations in standard approaches. Xie introduces the Q-target framework, which unifies existing supervised fine-tuning variants by decomposing the training objective into explicit choices about target probability distributions, specifically controlling how strongly to rely on observed tokens and how to allocate probability mass over alternative tokens. Building on this framework, he has developed TARGET-SFT, a practical method that consistently outperforms existing approaches across diverse dataset-model settings by better balancing dataset imitation, prior preservation, and alternative supervision. His research provides fundamental design principles that improve both the performance and training efficiency of language models by reconceptualizing supervised fine-tuning as a target distribution design problem rather than a simple imitation task.
