## Overview


<!-- bio-agent -->
Sohyun An is a researcher at the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA) who specializes in large language model optimization and post-training methodologies. An contributes to foundational work on supervised fine-tuning through the development of the Q-target framework, which reinterprets SFT as a target distribution design problem rather than a standard token prediction task. The Q-target framework decomposes fine-tuning supervision into explicit design choices governing how strongly to rely on observed tokens and how to allocate probability mass over alternative tokens, unifying many existing SFT variants under a common mathematical lens. An's TARGET-SFT method, grounded in this framework, demonstrates consistent empirical improvements across diverse dataset-model settings by balancing dataset imitation, prior preservation, and alternative supervision. This work provides both practical improvements to language model training and a more fundamental design principle for understanding the landscape of supervised fine-tuning approaches.
