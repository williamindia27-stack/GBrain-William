## Overview


<!-- bio-agent -->
Mengdi Wang is a researcher at Princeton University working in machine learning and adaptive systems for foundation models. Her research addresses the critical challenge of deploying models that must handle diverse, evolving task streams by developing test-time learning frameworks that avoid catastrophic interference across domains. In her work on Eevee, she pioneered a router-prompt co-evolution strategy that enables self-improving agents to selectively adapt their behavior to heterogeneous input streams while maintaining performance across multiple benchmarks, achieving cumulative retention gains of +41.53 compared to negative retention in competing approaches. Her contributions extend beyond individual benchmarks to practical deployment scenarios where foundation models must refine their responses through real-world interaction without explicit retraining. Wang's research vision centers on creating adaptive systems that can safely and efficiently improve themselves across shifting task distributions, enabling more robust and deployable AI agents in production environments.
