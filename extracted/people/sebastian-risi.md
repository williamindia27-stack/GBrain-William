## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Sebastian Risi is a researcher at Sakana AI working in reinforcement learning and language model optimization. He contributes to advancing test-time search capabilities through novel training approaches that improve model performance on complex reasoning tasks. His work on Vector Policy Optimization demonstrates how training models to produce diverse solutions across multiple reward dimensions can outperform standard scalar baselines, particularly as candidate budgets increase. Risi's research addresses a fundamental challenge in language model post-training: overcoming low-entropy response distributions that limit search procedures' ability to explore solution spaces effectively. His collaborative work shows that diversity-aware training unlocks solutions to problems that conventional methods cannot solve, with applications spanning coding, reasoning, navigation, and tool use tasks. This research direction reflects a broader vision of making language models more effective at exploration-exploitation tradeoffs critical to real-world problem solving.
