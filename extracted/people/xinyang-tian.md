## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Xinyang Tian is a researcher at Tsinghua University working in the intersection of neuroscience and machine learning, with a focus on developing interpretable representations of brain activity. He proposes novel approaches to electroencephalogram analysis through the development of microstate tokenizers that convert continuous EEG signals into discrete, interpretable patterns using k-means clustering. His work demonstrates that microstate representations significantly outperform traditional time-domain and frequency-domain features across multiple clinical and cognitive tasks including sleep staging, emotion recognition, and motor imagery classification. Tian's research shows that microstate tokenizers initialized on one task can generalize universally to downstream applications, addressing critical challenges of data scarcity in neuroimaging research. His work advances both the practical accuracy of EEG-based classification systems and the scientific understanding of how discrete brain states relate to cognitive function, with implications for clinical neuroscience and brain-computer interface development.
