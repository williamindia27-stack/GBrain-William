## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Marc Habermann is a computer scientist at the Max Planck Institute specializing in neural rendering and digital human synthesis. His research focuses on bridging the gap between photorealistic quality and computational efficiency in animatable avatar creation, as demonstrated in his work on MUA (Mobile Ultra-detailed Animatable Avatars), which employs wavelet-guided multi-level spatial factorized blendshape representations to achieve dramatic compression gains. He develops novel distillation pipelines that transfer high-fidelity geometric deformations and appearance details from teacher models into compact student representations, enabling real-time performance on mobile platforms including VR headsets. His work achieves unprecedented efficiency gains, reducing computational cost by up to 2000× and model size by 10× while maintaining visual fidelity comparable to server-based approaches. Habermann's research advances practical immersive computing by making photorealistic digital humans accessible on consumer devices, significantly enhancing the feasibility of immersive applications in virtual and mixed reality environments.
