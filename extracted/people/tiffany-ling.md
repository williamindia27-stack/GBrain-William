## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Tiffany Ling is a researcher at Carnegie Mellon University working in computer vision and multimodal learning, with a focus on video-language understanding and generation. She contributes to the CHAI framework, which develops structured specifications for precise video description in collaboration with professional video creators. Her work demonstrates how critique-based human-AI oversight can improve video captioning by leveraging model-generated pre-captions and expert human feedback to produce detailed post-captions. Through this approach, she shows that open-source vision-language models can be fine-tuned to match or exceed closed-source alternatives when trained with preference and critique supervision on videos with cinematically rich annotations. Her research enables professional-grade text-to-video generation with fine-grained control over motion, camera dynamics, and visual composition, advancing the precision and practical applicability of video content creation tools.
