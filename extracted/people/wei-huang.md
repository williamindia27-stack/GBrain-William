## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Wei Huang is a researcher specializing in artificial intelligence and world modeling, with expertise in designing systems that enable agents to understand and interact with complex environments. He contributes to foundational frameworks for agentic world modeling, including the development of the levels×laws taxonomy that categorizes world model capabilities across three levels (Predictor, Simulator, and Evolver) and four governing-law regimes (physical, digital, social, and scientific). His work synthesizes over 400 studies and analyzes more than 100 representative systems spanning model-based reinforcement learning, video generation, web and GUI agents, multi-agent social simulation, and AI-driven scientific discovery. Huang's research addresses critical evaluation challenges by proposing decision-centric evaluation principles and a minimal reproducible evaluation package designed to assess world models across different capability-regime pairs and identify failure modes. His work establishes a common language across fragmented research communities and provides architectural guidance for advancing world models from passive next-step prediction toward systems capable of simulating and reshaping environments, directly supporting long-horizon agentic reasoning and autonomous goal accomplishment.
