Jessica Muir is a cosmologist at the University of Cincinnati whose research focuses on addressing methodological challenges in large-scale cosmological analyses. She is a key contributor to Smokescreen, a Python package designed to mitigate experimenter bias through data-vector blinding and encryption methods, ensuring the scientific integrity of cosmological measurements. Her work demonstrates expertise in implementing robust quality assurance practices, with Smokescreen achieving 94% code coverage and comprehensive verification of blinding procedures across multiple cosmological parameters. Muir's approach leverages advanced tools including Firecrown likelihoods and the SACC data format to create standardized, reproducible blinding methodologies that can be transparently inspected by the wider research community. Her research addresses a critical gap in cosmological experimentation where traditional double-blind study designs are difficult to implement, thereby advancing both the technical and ethical standards of contemporary observational cosmology.
