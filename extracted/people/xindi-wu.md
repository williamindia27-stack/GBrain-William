## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Xindi Wu is a researcher at NVIDIA working in machine learning and computational efficiency, with a focus on diffusion models and statistical estimation. Wu co-develops CARV, a hierarchical Monte Carlo estimator designed to reduce variance in downstream tasks that employ pretrained diffusion models as teachers, achieving 2-3× effective compute multipliers in text-to-3D distillation and attribution experiments. The method combines amortized upstream computation over diffusion-noise resamples with timestep importance sampling and stratified-inverse-CDF construction, grounded in a compute-aware variance-accounting framework that guides practical estimator design. In single-step distillation tasks, Wu's techniques reduce gradient variance by an order of magnitude, demonstrating the broad applicability of variance reduction strategies across different distillation regimes. Wu's research addresses the practical problem of making large-scale diffusion model applications computationally tractable, with potential cost savings in the six to seven figure range that could enable wider deployment of these models across applications.
