## Overview


<!-- bio-agent -->
Pavel Osinenko is a researcher in reinforcement learning and control systems at the Center for Engineering Systems and Sciences and Sirius University of Science and Technology. His work focuses on improving the efficiency of reinforcement learning training by leveraging existing suboptimal policies through novel integration techniques. In his research on agency transferring model-free policy enhancement, he developed an arbitration module that strategically governs the execution of actions between a learning policy and a baseline policy, gradually shifting control from the baseline to the learning agent throughout training. His method achieves competitive or superior performance compared to established algorithms such as SAC, PPO, and TD3 on continuous-control benchmarks while maintaining higher goal-reaching rates and producing standalone neural networks that operate without baseline support. His work demonstrates practical applications for domains like robotics, logistics, and finance where functional but suboptimal policies are available and can serve as foundations for learning improvements. Osinenko's research aims to make reinforcement learning more efficient and accessible by reducing training costs and accelerating convergence in real-world control problems.
