## Overview


<!-- bio-agent -->
Yunqi Hong is a machine learning researcher at UCLA whose work focuses on improving the post-training and fine-tuning of large language models through principled approaches to supervised learning. His research introduces the Q-target framework, which reinterprets supervised fine-tuning as an explicit target distribution design problem, decomposing SFT supervision into interpretable choices about how strongly to rely on observed tokens and how to allocate probability mass over alternative predictions. Through this framework, he demonstrates that many existing SFT variants can be understood as implicit choices within a unified design space that balances dataset imitation, prior preservation, and alternative supervision. Hong's empirical work on TARGET-SFT, developed collaboratively with colleagues at UCLA including Cho-Jui Hsieh, consistently outperforms baseline approaches across diverse dataset-model settings. His research contributes to more fundamental and efficient design principles for language model training, with practical implications for improving the quality and performance of fine-tuned models.
