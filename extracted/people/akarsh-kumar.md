## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Akarsh Kumar is a computer science researcher at MIT working at the intersection of reinforcement learning and language model optimization. He develops novel training approaches to improve how language models generate diverse candidate solutions, as demonstrated in his work on Vector Policy Optimization (VPO), which trains models to anticipate diverse reward functions and span the Pareto frontier of possible solutions. His research shows that VPO-trained models significantly outperform standard baselines on test-time search across multiple domains including multi-hop question answering, logic reasoning, coding tasks, and tool use. By combining multi-answer generation with stochastic reward scalarizations, Kumar addresses a critical limitation of current post-training methods: their tendency to produce low-entropy response distributions that constrain search effectiveness. His work has practical implications for improving exploration and exploitation in complex reasoning tasks where multiple valid solution paths exist.
