## Overview



<!-- bio-agent -->
Siyang Xue is a researcher at Tsinghua University working at the intersection of neuroscience and deep learning, with a focus on developing novel computational approaches to brain signal analysis. His work centers on the development of microstate tokenizers that transform continuous electroencephalogram signals into discrete, interpretable representations through k-means clustering, enabling more robust and scalable EEG analysis. In his 2025 paper "Atoms Of Thought: Universal EEG Representation Learning With Microstates," Xue demonstrates that microstate-based representations substantially outperform traditional time-domain and frequency-domain features across diverse clinical applications including sleep staging, emotion recognition, and motor imagery classification. His key contribution is establishing that microstate tokenizers initialized on one task can generalize universally to downstream applications, addressing fundamental challenges in EEG analysis related to artifact susceptibility, data scarcity, and feature interpretability. Through this work, Xue advances the theoretical understanding of brain microstates as fundamental units of neural computation while simultaneously creating practical tools for clinical neuroscience and brain-computer interface development.
