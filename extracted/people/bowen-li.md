Bowen Li is a computer science researcher contributing to the development of advanced AI agent systems and software engineering tools. He is a core contributor to OpenHands, a comprehensive platform that enables artificial intelligence agents to write code, interact with command-line interfaces, and browse the web through a modular event stream architecture and sandboxed runtime environments. His work on OpenHands encompasses the implementation of multiple specialized and generalist agents, as well as evaluation frameworks that assess agent performance across challenging tasks including software engineering benchmarks like SWE-BENCH and web interaction tasks like WEBARENA. Through his contributions to this community-driven platform that has attracted over 2.1K contributions from 188 collaborators, Li has helped establish foundational infrastructure for developing AI agents capable of complex interactions with computing environments. His research vision centers on enabling AI systems to perform sophisticated software development and engineering tasks while maintaining safety and reliability in sandboxed deployment contexts.
